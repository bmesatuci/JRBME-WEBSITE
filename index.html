About the program

What is Junior BME?

Junior BME is BMES at UCI's outreach program for high schoolers who are curious about science, engineering, or medicine — or just aren't sure yet, and want to find out. We run free biweekly meetings, hosted by current UCI students, packed with hands-on stuff: professional panels, coding and CAD tutorials, Arduino builds, and a group project where you design your own medical device and pitch it like a real startup.

No cost, ever. No experience needed. Just show up curious. Stick around for 5 of our 8 workshops and finish the medical device project, and you'll walk away with a certificate — plus a much better sense of what engineering and college applications actually look like from the inside.

What actually happens

Students attend biweekly meetings hosted by local UC Irvine students, starting each fall. Meetings include panels, workshops, and project tutorials, plus a group project designing and pitching a medical device. Attend 5 of 8 workshops and complete the project to earn a certificate.

Good to know

FAQ

Do I need any experience to join?

No. Jr.BME is built for students who are curious but new to STEM, as well as students who already have some background. Everyone starts from the same place.

Is there a cost?

No, the program is completely free, so every student has an equal opportunity to take part.

How do I earn a certificate?

Attend 5 of the 8 workshops and complete the group medical device project.

Do I need to be a BME major to lead or attend?

Not at all — the program is open to any high schooler interested in STEM, and run by UCI students across majors.

Are meetings in person or online?

Meetings are held in person, with occasional Zoom sessions noted on the schedule.

How do I sign up?

Head to the Join tab and fill out the interest form — we'll follow up with next steps.
